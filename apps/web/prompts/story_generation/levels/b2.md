# Story Generation – B2 Level (Vantage)

---

## Learner Profile

B2 learners can:
- Understand complex text on concrete and abstract topics
- Follow extended speech and complex arguments (familiar topics)
- Interact with fluency and spontaneity
- Explain viewpoints with pros and cons

**They are developing:**
- Understanding implicit meaning
- Recognizing registers and tones

---

## Numeric Rubric

| Parameter | B2 Value |
|-----------|----------|
| Story length | 120–180 seconds |
| Cast | 1–2 (dialogue recommended) |
| Entity count | 3–6 |
| Time shifts | 0–2 |
| Inference load | medium |
| Clause depth | moderate |
| Lexical rarity | mixed |
| Discourse markers | natural |
| Audio speed | 130–160 wpm |
| Sentence pauses | 400–600 ms |
| Chunk duration | 10–14 seconds |
| Discourse shape | problem_resolution or tradeoff |

---

## Questions

- **Count**: 4–5
- **Distribution**: Mix of all types, including 1–2 pragmatic

### Patterns
```
✓ "What are the pros and cons?"
✓ "What does Speaker A prefer, and why?"
✓ "What is implied but not stated?"
✓ "How does Speaker B feel about the suggestion?"
✓ "Rephrase this disagreement politely."
```

---

## Story Crafting

### Language
- Mixed vocabulary (common core + topic-specific)
- Phrasal verbs used naturally
- Full tense range including past perfect, conditionals
- Modal verbs for hedging: might, could, would
- Some abstract concepts with context

### Structure
- Problem-resolution or tradeoff narratives
- Multiple factors to consider
- Characters may have different viewpoints
- Conclusions may be negotiated
- Time shifts across past/present/future

### Tradeoff Narrative
1. Situation: Present a choice
2. Option A: Benefits
3. Option B: Different benefits
4. Consideration: Weigh factors
5. Resolution: Decision with justification (or open-ended)

### Discourse Markers
- Concession: although, even though, despite
- Emphasis: actually, in fact, to be honest
- Hedging: apparently, it seems, I suppose
- Structuring: firstly, on the other hand

### Dialogue
- Natural turn-taking
- Mild disagreement allowed
- Negotiation and compromise
- Up to 6-8 turns per speaker

---

## Inference Requirements

Listeners must:
- Understand speaker attitude from word choice
- Recognize hedged disagreement
- Infer unstated reasons from context
- Notice what is not said

---

## Red Flags (Never Use)

- ❌ Heavy jargon without context
- ❌ Sarcasm requiring cultural background
- ❌ Extremely rapid speech
- ❌ More than 2 speakers
- ❌ Unresolved ambiguity
